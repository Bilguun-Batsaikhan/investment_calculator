How the Calculation Works

This investment calculator operates on an annual compounding basis, adding contributions at the end of each year. Here's a breakdown of the key calculations performed for each year:

Interest Earned in Year:

This is calculated by multiplying the investmentValue at the beginning of the year by the expectedReturn rate.

Interest Earned = Current Investment Value × (Expected Return / 100)

Investment Value at End of Year:

The investmentValue is updated by adding the interestEarnedInYear and the annualInvestment (your new contribution for that year). This new value becomes the starting point for the next year's calculation.

Value End of Year = Starting Investment Value + Interest Earned + Annual Investment

Total Amount Invested:

This simply tracks the cumulative amount of money you have personally contributed.

Total Amount Invested = Initial Investment + (Annual Investment × Current Year)

Total Interest Earned:

This represents the total profit your investment has generated up to that point. It's the valueEndOfYear minus all the money you've put in.

Total Interest = Value End of Year - Total Amount Invested
